Questions clients already ask before they call.

Can short-term rental losses offset W-2 income?

Sometimes. The result depends on the property facts, average guest stay, material participation, documentation, and how the activity is reported.

When should a real estate investor talk to a CPA?

Before buying, selling, renovating, converting to short-term rental use, or changing entities. The best tax choices are usually made before the transaction closes.

What does proactive tax planning include?

A review of income, entities, properties, deductions, retirement accounts, timing opportunities, and the records needed to defend the strategy.

Searchable answers

Built around investor queries.

Do I need real estate professional status?
It depends on the loss type, participation, time records, and whether the rental is treated as a passive activity.
Should my rental activity be in an LLC?
That is both a legal and tax question. The tax answer depends on ownership, financing, entity elections, and the long-term plan.
Is a short-term rental different from a long-term rental?
Yes. Average guest stay and services provided can affect how the activity is treated for tax purposes.

Helpful answers

Plain-English pages clients can actually use.

What changes when I buy my first rental?

Your records, deduction categories, depreciation, financing documents, and estimated taxes may all need attention.

Can my CPA help before I sell?

Yes. Sale timing, basis, depreciation, gain, and reinvestment options are better reviewed before the contract is final.

Is tax planning only for business owners?

No. Planning can help W-2 earners, retirees, equity-comp clients, and real estate investors avoid avoidable surprises.

What should I bring to a planning consult?

Prior returns, income estimates, property details, entity documents, and the decisions you are considering this year.
